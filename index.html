The problem

Safety information lives everywhere — and nowhere.

A pothole on your street is talked about in a WhatsApp group. A power outage is reported on Twitter with the wrong location. A theft happens in your area and the neighbors only hear about it days later.

There's no shared, real-time picture of what's safe and what isn't. Safe IQ is being built to be that picture — owned by the community, not by any platform.

What's inside

A safety toolkit designed for real neighborhoods.

Report in 30 seconds

Pick a category, drop a location pin, snap up to 5 photos, add a quick note. Done. Your report is live for everyone in the area.

Real-time nearby alerts

See a live map of what's happening around you. Critical hazards bubble up to the top of your feed. Get push notifications only for your area — no spam from across the city.

One-tap SOS

Emergency? Send a beacon to the nearby community with your location, in one press. No fumbling through menus. When it matters most, it's already ready.

Verified by the community

Every report gets a confidence score based on how many neighbors confirm it. Admin-verified reports get a special badge. No fake panic. No sensationalism.

My parking

Save where you parked. Multiple spots supported. Directions back to your car open in Google Maps. Because "which floor was it?" shouldn't be a mystery.

AI safety assistant

Ask questions about safety in your area, or get a plain-language summary of what's happening nearby. Powered by Gemini AI with your location context.

How it works

Three moves. Everyone safer.

1

You see something

A pothole, a leaking transformer, an unsafe spot at night. Open Safe IQ, tap Report.

2

The area gets alerted

Everyone within your neighborhood sees the report on their live map. Critical hazards trigger push notifications.

3

The community verifies

Neighbors confirm or reject. Confidence builds. Real problems get attention. Fake ones get filtered out.

Under the hood

Built with intent. Every choice earned.

Safe IQ is a solo project by a self-taught Android developer — from Kotlin all the way to Firestore security rules. Every piece of the stack was chosen deliberately, and the whole thing runs on the free tier of every service so the community never pays a rupee.

Client

Jetpack Compose — 100% modern Kotlin UI

  • Compose BOM 2024.05, Kotlin 1.9.24, AGP 8.7.2
  • Material 3 theming, edge-to-edge, dark mode default
  • Hilt (kapt) for dependency injection
  • Room 2.6 for local caching where it counts

Maps & location

Google Maps SDK + FusedLocationProvider

  • maps-compose 4.4, marker clustering
  • Dynamic map padding tracks bottom-sheet position
  • Auto-recenter on user bearing after gesture idle
  • Places SDK autocomplete with debounced session tokens

Backend

Firebase Spark (free) tier — forever

  • Auth: email/password + Google Sign-In
  • Firestore: reports, users, verifications, comments
  • Cloud Messaging: geohash-topic push notifications
  • Security rules with email-allowlist admin check

Media

ImgBB for photo hosting

  • Firebase Storage requires Blaze — we don't pay
  • Free public image host, permanent URLs
  • JPEG compressed to 1600px, EXIF rotation applied
  • Up to 5 photos per report, batched upload

Intelligence

Google Gemini for the AI Assistant

  • Direct REST API, no Cloud Functions needed
  • Streaming responses with retry-on-failure
  • Location + report context injected in prompt
  • Voice: speech recognition + TTS + NLP parser

Privacy

You own your data. Full stop.

  • No analytics SDK. No ads SDK. No third-party tracking.
  • Location used only for map centering & nearby alerts
  • Reports are visible to anyone signed in, by design
  • Personal info stays on your device unless YOU post it

Common questions

Frequently asked.

Is Safe IQ available on iOS?

Not yet. Android is the primary platform for now — it's where most of our target community already lives. iOS is on the long-term roadmap if Android traction validates the model.

How do you keep costs at zero?

Every service is chosen for its free tier. Firebase Spark plan (Auth, Firestore, FCM), ImgBB for image hosting, direct Gemini REST for AI. No paid Cloud Functions, no paid Storage. Google Maps has a generous free quota that easily covers organic launch usage.

Who moderates the reports?

The community verifies first — every report has a confidence score based on nearby users who confirm or reject it. On top of that, an admin panel lets us verify high-impact reports and hide the obvious noise. Delete-with-cascade is one tap for admins.

Is my location data safe?

Location is used only for centering the map and for filtering the nearby-alerts feed. It never leaves your device unless YOU decide to post a report at that location. There's no analytics SDK, no ads SDK, no third-party tracking anywhere in the app.

Can I contribute or fork the concept?

The client-side is not currently open-source but that's under review. Reach out via Instagram or email if you're a developer interested in helping, or a researcher/professor who wants a technical walkthrough.
